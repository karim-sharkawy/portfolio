# How Mathematicians Think — William Byers

A profound exploration of the creative and intuitive processes behind mathematical discovery. Byers examines how mathematicians approach problems, emphasizing the role of imagination, analogy, and aesthetic judgment in mathematical thinking.

## Key Insights
- Mathematics as a creative endeavor
- The importance of intuition in problem-solving
- How mathematical beauty guides discovery

## Why I Read It
This book deepened my understanding of mathematical creativity and the non-algorithmic aspects of mathematical work, which complements my research in optimization and atmospheric modeling.
