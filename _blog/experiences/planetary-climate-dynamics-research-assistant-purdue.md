# Planetary Climate Dynamics Research Assistant | Purdue University (05/2022–12/2022)

## Overview
Studied Martian climate dynamics under Professor Lei Wang, focusing on storm track variability and annular modes in the Earth, Atmospheric, and Planetary Sciences department.

## Key Responsibilities and Achievements

- **Data Analysis**: Analyzed 90GB reanalysis datasets (EMARS, MACDA) in netCDF format using Python to research dust storm track variability.

- **Workflow Development**: Developed data processing workflows and visualizations to support atmospheric dynamics research and reporting.

- **Research Focus**: Investigated midlatitude eddy activity and its interaction with Martian topography, drawing connections to Rossby wave dynamics and atmospheric circulation on Earth and Titan.

- **Dissemination**: Presented findings to the research team and contributed an abstract submission to the AGU (American Geophysical Union) conference.

## Theoretical Connections
- Worked with linearized equations for Rossby waves
- Linked wave propagation and instability to observed storm track behavior
- Contextualized findings within planetary science frameworks

## Technologies Used

- Visualization tools (Panoply)
- Atmospheric modeling concepts
