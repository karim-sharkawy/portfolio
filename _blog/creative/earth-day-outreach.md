# Volunteering Adventures with Purdue EAPS

While I was studying at Purdue, I had the chance to volunteer in a few different ways through the Earth, Atmospheric, and Planetary Sciences (EAPS) program. Each opportunity was unique, but both gave me a deeper appreciation for teaching, curiosity, and the joy of hands-on learning.

One of my favorite experiences was participating in Earth Day outreach for elementary school students. We designed interactive activities to make environmental science tangible and fun. I helped teach kids about energy conservation and simple ways to reduce household carbon footprints. The goal wasn’t just to convey facts—it was to inspire small, meaningful actions that students could take at home. Seeing their eyes light up when they realized they could make a difference was incredibly rewarding.

Another experience involved creating a video about exoplanet “barcodes” to help students understand how scientists detect and analyze distant planetary atmospheres. The concept could have been abstract and intimidating, but by turning it into a visual, story-driven lesson, we made it approachable and engaging. It was fascinating to see how complex scientific ideas can be broken down into something fun and memorable for students, sparking curiosity about space and science in ways that stick.

Both of these experiences reinforced an important lesson: teaching is not just about delivering information—it’s about sparking curiosity, making science accessible, and showing students that learning can be interactive and exciting. I walked away from each project inspired by the creativity and eagerness of young minds, and reminded of why sharing knowledge matters so much.

Check out the [Earth Day outreach](https://www.linkedin.com/in/karimme/details/volunteer-experiences/edit/forms/1028607703/) and the [Exoplanet Barcodes video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sPoPixYEbCA) to see some of the fun ways we brought science to life.
