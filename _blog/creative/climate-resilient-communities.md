# Facilitating Climate Workshops in Toronto

One of the highlights of my work in climate education was traveling to Toronto for the first time—my very first trip to Canada! I had the chance to contribute as a speaker and workshop facilitator for youth-focused programming with Climate Resilient Communities, and it ended up being one of those experiences that sticks with you.

The workshops were designed to make climate issues tangible and relevant to the participants’ lives. I helped co-create sessions that combined discussions, interactive activities, and local climate data. One exercise that always stands out involved students using simple, accessible tools to measure aspects of their local environment and then turning that data into a story about their community. I remember one group creating a “climate diary” for their neighborhood, noting how small actions like planting trees or reducing waste could add up—and they were so excited to share their findings with everyone.

We also gamified some activities to spark creativity and collaboration. In one challenge, students competed to design the most impactful community action plan based on real climate data. The energy in the room was contagious, and the way they approached the task—combining humor, clever ideas, and data-driven reasoning—was inspiring.

A key part of the workshops was centering narratives on marginalized communities and showing how local climate action can address real social challenges. The students quickly grasped that climate work isn’t just abstract—it’s about people, equity, and creating change in the places they care about.

Being in Toronto added an extra layer of excitement. Exploring a new city while facilitating these workshops made the experience feel like an adventure, and seeing youth get genuinely excited about using data and storytelling to take action made it unforgettable.

This experience reinforced a lesson I carry into all my work: bridging technical tools with community-driven knowledge isn’t just valuable—it’s essential. When people feel empowered to contribute their own insights, science and data come alive in ways that truly matter.
