# Linear Programming Research Assistant | Purdue University (06/2023–12/2024)

## Overview
Worked under Professor Thomas Sinclair on investigating positive linear mappings and their extensions through theoretical analysis and computational experimentation.

## Key Responsibilities and Achievements

- **Computational Tools Development**: Developed Python-based tools using linear programming to construct positive mappings under feasibility constraints and SVMs to classify mappings at scale.

- **Classifier Implementation**: Implemented and benchmarked scikit-learn classifiers to study separability and decision boundaries in high dimensions, tested on 100K data points.

- **Theoretical Contributions**: Applied convex optimization methods to improve interpretability in structured classification tasks. Proved properties of liftable and extendable mappings, including that positive matrices are always extendable.

- **Research Dissemination**: Co-authored 2 forthcoming papers and presented results to 40+ faculty and students at a Purdue mathematics seminar.

## Theoretical Foundations
- Worked extensively with Farkas' Lemma and hyperplane separation theorem
- Connected convex geometry with optimization theory
- Explored feasibility, duality, and constraint geometry

## Technologies Used

- Linear Programming solvers
