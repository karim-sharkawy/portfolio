---

In the beginning, the universe was formless.
Then came symmetry—breaking it.

---

A crystal grows in perfect fractals,
each layer a mirror of intention.
But observe closely: no two snowflakes match.

The deviation makes it beautiful.

---

I think machine learning is this—
patterns and their breaks,
the tension between what should be
and what surprises us anyway.

We train on symmetry,
then pray our systems
don't repeat it too perfectly.

---

A sphere is perfectly symmetric.
Roll it down the hill—
the path remains unmarked.

A ball with a scratch
writes its story in sand.

---

Maybe intelligence
is learning where the symmetries end.
Knowing when to trust them.
Knowing when to break them.

---

In code, we build walls of logic.
Clean. Symmetrical. Safe.

Then real data arrives—
asymmetrical, noisy, alive—
and the system must dance
with what it cannot fully predict.

There is grace in that.

---

*"In physics, to formalize a symmetry is to explain why it was there. 
To break it is to explain what matters."* — some physicist I misremembered
